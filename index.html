Stability Diagnostics A family of instruments

One governance law, read six ways.

Most monitoring watches a machine fail. Three of these instruments read its stability before the failure is visible; the fourth reads how efficiently it moves; the fifth runs the law across an entire operating fleet, live; the sixth reads the same law in the instructions that drive AI systems. All six come from a single idea: a system stays stable, and moves economically, only while its absorption capacity keeps up with the load driving it. Each one reads that balance from a different angle, and each is grounded in real, public data.

Θ = I / R driving load over absorption capacity · stable while Θ < 1

INSTRUMENT 01
When
Cross-domain early warning
Projects when the binding resource runs out, by tracking which capacity is closest to exhaustion across every domain at once, not one at a time.
One detector, three machines.
Real Li-ion battery & bearing; turbofan benchmark.

A second branch
The first three predict failure. This one finds waste.
The same law has a second use. Instead of asking when a machine breaks, it asks how much energy it burns to do its job, and where that energy is thrown away. Efficiency is the governance ratio minimized rather than monitored.
INSTRUMENT 04
How well
Cost of transport
Measures how efficiently a machine moves, and shows that choosing the right gait for each speed minimizes the energy cost of transport, which is the same load-over-capacity ratio.
Efficiency, not failure.
Real measured quadruped power; energy-minimizing gaits on a real robot.

A third branch
One instrument, one whole fleet.
The same exhaustion detector, applied unchanged to every unit of a real operating constellation at once. Atmospheric drag is the driving load, orbit maintenance is the capacity, and each satellite sits somewhere on the corridor between them.
INSTRUMENT 05
Which
Fleet capacity radar: Starlink
Reads the governance state of 10,000+ satellites at once from public orbital elements, and ranks who is closest to capacity exhaustion on a live reentry corridor.
Validated early warning.
Real archived orbital histories; 21 of 21 deorbits detected, 0 of 10 false alarms over 13 months, median 167 days of warning before burn-up.

A fourth branch
The same law on a different substrate: instructions, not machines.
Compression has a load and a capacity too: the critical intent an instruction carries, against the token budget left after compression. This instrument measures where that channel fails, not a machine.
INSTRUMENT 06
How much
Compression threshold gauge
Measures how much an instruction can be compressed before a safety constraint silently inverts, rather than degrades visibly.
Constraint survival, not just savings.
Real 1,104 executions, three compressors vs. a random control; 92-100% of negation constraints silently inverted at typical compression; 204 of 246 breaks traced to channel deletion.

The instruments answer different questions, but they are not six ideas. They are one law, Θ = I/R, instantiated six times. The same balance that says when a resource will run out also says where coupling concentrates, whether a system is sliding toward instability, how efficiently it can move, which unit of a whole fleet is next to leave its envelope, and how much an instruction can be compressed before its meaning inverts. Each page states its scope plainly, including its honest limits.
